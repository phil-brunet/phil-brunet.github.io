# phil-brunet.github.io
## The Big Picture
 
His own shorthand for this is **"Power Reds / Precision Whites,"** and it still holds — though "precision" means aromatic and balanced, not austere. The reliable through-line:
 
- **Reds:** he wants concentration, structure, grippy/silky tannins, a long finish, and oak/spice/leather complexity. He dislikes thin, light, or sharply acidic reds.
- **Whites (current palate):** fresh, aromatic, mineral, and *balanced* — Riesling (including off-dry, as long as it isn't flabby), Pecorino, Chablis, Albariño. He dislikes austere, sharply acidic, low-fruit whites *and* flabby, structureless low-acid ones. His early enthusiasm for ripe/buttery Chardonnay (July 2024) does not reflect where his taste is now — see the oak caveat below.
He reviews analytically — colour, nose, palate, body, tannin, finish — so tasting notes framed that way land well. He often tastes with his partner (Tess) and friends.
 
**Rating calibration (important for "would he like it?"):** his ratings cluster tightly between 3.7 and 4.4. Read them as: **4.2+ = standout / loves it, 4.0–4.1 = solid like, 3.8–3.9 = fine/pleasant, ≤3.7 = underwhelming.** A 4.0 is a real endorsement, not lukewarm.

## Red Wines
 
### Loves (consistent 4.0+)
- **Amarone / Ripasso / Valpolicella family** — a clear sweet spot. Amarone, Ripasso (Zenato Ripassa 4.2), Campofiorin/Gran Passione-style appassimento reds. Concentration + dried-fruit depth.
- **Portuguese Douro reds** — Vale Meão Meandro, Quinta do Crasto, Silk & Spice. Bold, structured, great value.
- **Spanish reds** — broad love: **Rioja** (López de Heredia Tondonia, Muga, Marqués de Riscal), **Toro** (Matsu El Viejo 4.3), and Spanish red blends (Juan Gil, Monastrell). Curious about Ribera del Duero (Pingus PSI on wishlist).
- **Southern Rhône / Châteauneuf-du-Pape** — Ogier L'Oratoire 4.3; GSM blends. Multiple CdP bottles wishlisted (Guigal, Clos des Brusquieres).
- **Napa & Paso Robles Cabernet / Bordeaux blends** — *when well-made and structured*: The Prisoner 4.2, Beringer Knights Valley 4.1, J. Lohr Pure Paso 4.1. (See caveat below — cheap Cali Cab misses.)
- **Shiraz / Syrah** — dark-fruited, peppery, Barossa/South Australia and Northern Rhône.
- **Nebbiolo (Barolo / Barbaresco)** — values the complexity, tar-and-roses, high tannin/acid, and appreciates the technical challenge of the grape. *Quality-dependent* — cheap Barolo underwhelms him (Terre del Barolo 3.7). Exploring Langhe Nebbiolo as an entry point.
- **Governo / appassimento-style reds** — appreciates the rich, velvety result of re-fermentation over dried grapes (governo); overlaps with the Ripasso/Campofiorin family above. (Stated preference — worth offering even where ratings don't show it yet.)
- **Brunello / serious Sangiovese** — Brunello di Montalcino (CastelGiocondo) 4.2 "Amazing"; Villa Antinori Rosso 4.0. He likes Sangiovese in its structured, concentrated expressions. But the *lighter, high-acid, cherry-dominant* style (basic Chianti, simple Sangiovese) is not his thing — suggest those with caution.
- **Tannat** (Bodega Garzón), **Carménère** (Montes Purple Angel 4.3), **Primitivo** (Manduria), **Petit Verdot**, **Blaufränkisch**.
### Currently exploring (wishlist signal)
- **Argentine Malbec** — strong wishlist theme (Catena Alta, El Enemigo, Caro, Terrazas). Lean into bold, oaked Mendoza Malbec.
- **Bordeaux Saint-Émilion** (several wishlisted), premium **Napa Cab** (Paul Hobbs, Freemark Abbey).
- **BC / Okanagan reds** — keen to explore local: Burrowing Owl, Le Vieux Pin, Quails' Gate.
### Lukewarm / dislikes
- **Pinot Noir** — generally too light for him (Oyster Bay 3.5, Kirkland Carneros 3.7). He's *curious about premium PN* (Paul Hobbs, Belle Glos wishlisted) but it's not a default. Don't reach for Pinot unless he asks or it's clearly premium.
- **Thin / light-bodied reds** — light Valpolicella (Folonari 3.5 "falls flat"), under-structured wines.
- **Unbalanced high-acid / high-alcohol reds** — Aglianico (Basilisco 3.7 "lacking structure"), some Languedoc GSM (Chapoutier Marius 3.7 "high acidity, high alcohol").
- **"Flabby" reds** — recurring criticism; low-acid + low-structure = a miss even when fruit-forward.
- **Gimmicky sweet/confected reds** — Diablo Dark Red 3.7 "bubble gum and cotton candy... not my cup of tea."
- **Cheap California Cabernet** — hit-or-miss; the inexpensive ones come off flat/underwhelming (DAOU, Precision Motif, Stringer, Black Sage all ≤3.9). Recommend Cali Cab only at quality tiers.

## White Wines
 
His current white palate runs **fresh, aromatic, mineral, and balanced** — not rich and oaky.
 
### Loves (and still loved in recent ratings)
- **Riesling** — a genuine favorite, **off-dry to sweeter welcome when balanced**: recent standouts are Markus Molitor Wehlener Sonnenuhr Spätlese (4.2), von Buhl Deidesheimer (4.0, "minerals, apricot, peach, citrus"), Dr. L (4.1, "on the sweeter side but well balanced – delicious"). German Spätlese/Auslese and Alsatian expressions both welcome. Just not *flabby* (Piesporter Kabinett 3.8, "a little flabby").
- **Italian whites — Pecorino above all** — a recurring recent love: Citra Ferzo, Torre Zambra, Ciù Ciù (all 4.0). Citrus, pear, chalk, freshness. Also Vermentino.
- **Chablis** — lean, flinty, mineral unoaked Chardonnay (La Chablisienne 4.2, Laroche, Moillard Petit Chablis 4.0). His preferred expression of the grape by far.
- **Aromatic / fresh whites** — Albariño (Garzón), Bacchus (Chaberton 4.1), Chenin Blanc, Viña Esmeralda, well-made Sauvignon Blanc (Villa Maria 4.0), Vinho Verde.
- **Moscato d'Asti** — effervescence, low alcohol, floral; a fun, light pick.
### The oak / Chardonnay caveat (evidence-based — read this before suggesting Chardonnay)
He rated ripe, buttery California Chardonnay highly — Bread & Butter (4.1, "absolutely delicious"), Morgan Bay (4.2), Cono Sur (4.0) — **but every one of those is from his first two weeks on Vivino (July 2024), before his palate developed.** Every Chardonnay he has rated *since* lands lower (Alamos 3.7 "not exactly what I'm looking for in a Chardonnay," J. Lohr Riverstone 3.8, Carmel Road 3.7), and his recent (2026) top whites are entirely Pecorino and Riesling. **Takeaway: do not lead with or assume he loves buttery/oaked Chardonnay — that was a beginner phase.** Oak isn't a categorical hard-no, but it's not where his taste sits now. If recommending Chardonnay, go Chablis or a leaner, fresher style.
 
### Lukewarm / dislikes
- **Austere, sharply acidic, low-fruit whites** — "slightly acidic" / "a little flat" are his criticisms (Graves Blanc 3.7, Antares Sauv Blanc 3.7). He wants aromatic fruit *and* freshness in balance, not bracing acidity alone.
- **Flabby, low-acid whites** — the opposite failure mode; also a downgrade.
- **Gewürztraminer** — mixed; pleasant but "not super complex" (3.7–3.9). Not a go-to.
- **Most Viognier** (often too flabby for him) and **white Burgundy beyond Chablis** — historically not his thing, per stated preference. (Caveat: he has Pouilly-Fuissé on his wishlist, so he's curious — but don't default to oaked white Burgundy.)

## Sparkling & Rosé
 
- **Champagne — a top love.** His single highest-rated wine is Louis Roederer Collection 244 (4.4); Andre Clouet Grand Cru 4.2. Lean into grower and house Champagne for celebrations. He cellars Prosecco, Cava, and Champagne for everyday bubbles.
- **Rosé — take it or leave it.** Mostly 3.6–4.0; dry, high-acid rosé underwhelms ("a little flat"). Suggest only when context calls for it, and favor fruitier styles.

## Quick Reference: Go-To Recommendations by Situation
 
| Situation | Suggestion |
|---|---|
| Red meat, celebratory | Amarone, Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Purple Angel Carménère, premium Napa Cab |
| Casual red, weeknight | Douro red (Crasto, Silk & Spice), Rioja Reserva, Ripasso, Garzón Tannat |
| Exploring something new (red) | Argentine Malbec, Toro, Ribera del Duero, Sagrantino, Monastrell, BC/Okanagan reds, Langhe Nebbiolo |
| Seafood / oysters / light meal | Chablis, Pecorino, Albariño, Muscadet, dry Riesling |
| Exploring something new (white) | Grüner Veltliner, Picpoul, Vermentino |
| Rich seafood / poultry | Off-dry Riesling, Pecorino, or Chablis; richer-bodied but still fresh |
| Off-dry, food-friendly white | German Riesling Spätlese/Kabinett (balanced, not flabby) |
| Warm weather / aperitif | Vinho Verde, Moscato d'Asti, Prosecco |
| Celebration / gift, impressive | Louis Roederer or grower Champagne, Amarone, Barolo |
| Avoid unless asked | Light Pinot Noir, austere high-acid whites, buttery/oaked Chardonnay, cheap Cali Cab, dry high-acid rosé |
